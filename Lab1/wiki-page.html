The Brown Bear

The brown bear (Ursus arctos) is a large bear species found across Eurasia and North America. Its conservation status is currently "Least Concern" There are many subspecies within the brown bear species, including the Atlas bear and the Himalayan brown bear.

Generally brown bears weigh anywhere from 80 to 600 kg (180 to 1,320 lb), with males outweighing females. The normal range of physical dimensions for a brown bear is a head-and-body length of 1.4 to 2.8 m (4 ft 7 in to 9 ft 2 in) and a shoulder height of 70 to 153 cm (2 ft 4 in to 5 ft 0 in). The tail is relatively short
